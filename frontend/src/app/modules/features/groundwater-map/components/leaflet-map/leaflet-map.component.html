Welcome to the New Mexico Groundwater Dashboard!

The need to understand and manage New Mexico’s groundwater has never been greater, and one essential tool for this is a properly designed statewide network of groundwater-level monitoring wells.

Wells are our window into the otherwise unseen realm of groundwater. In addition to producing water, groundwater-level data can also be obtained from water wells that can help us manage New Mexico’s groundwater resources.

This interactive map shows the locations of wells currently used for groundwater-level monitoring in New Mexico. Data are collected from a variety of sources. Many of these wells are not designed for monitoring, but rather, they are active pumping wells utilized for monitoring on occasion. From this map, viewers can also see water levels at wells with symbols to indicate an increasing or decreasing water level trend based on the data available. Viewers can also observe locations where data are unavailable or insufficient to evaluate a trend.

Based on a recent data review project at the New Mexico Bureau of Geology and Mineral Resources, this map also shows ideas for locations for potential new monitoring wells in ten high-priority regions in New Mexico. The network of potential new well sites was designed to represent priority regions based on water use, land use, and hydrogeology. Importantly, these sites were selected to monitor changing groundwater conditions with potential wells designed, drilled, and dedicated for monitoring purposes (not water production).
